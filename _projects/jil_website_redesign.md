{% capture summary %}
Justice Innovation Lab (JIL) needed a clearer, more accessible way to communicate its work and impact. We redesigned its website and brand system to improve usability, strengthen storytelling, and enable a small, non-design team to consistently maintain and evolve the experience.
{% endcapture %}

{% capture challenge %}
JIL’s website was a critical channel for sharing research, insights, and impact — but it wasn’t effectively serving its audience.

The site was difficult to navigate, the brand system lacked consistency, and accessibility issues limited how clearly JIL could present its data and insights. In particular, existing color palettes did not meet accessibility standards, and visual patterns risked unintentionally signaling political bias.

At the same time, JIL needed to launch a new “knowledge hub” — a centralized destination for reports, blog posts, and multimedia — within a tight six-week timeline. With no in-house design team, any solution also had to be easy for non-designers to use and maintain.

JIL needed a modern, accessible design system and website that could clearly communicate its work — and sustain itself over time.
{% endcapture %}

{% capture solution %}
We partnered with JIL to redesign its website and brand system using an agile, user-centered approach, delivering working improvements in two-week increments.

Our work focused on creating a cohesive, accessible, and sustainable design foundation:

- Simplified site architecture to make content easier to find and navigate  
- Designed a new knowledge hub with search, tagging, and filtering to organize reports, blog posts, and multimedia  
- Refreshed the visual identity, including accessible color palettes, typography, and design patterns aligned with WCAG standards  
- Improved data visualization guidance to better communicate insights while maintaining accessibility  
- Created modular, reusable templates to support consistent content creation  
- Developed clear brand and content guidelines so non-designers could confidently apply the system  

We worked closely with JIL throughout the process, iterating on designs through regular feedback sessions and ensuring the final system aligned with both user needs and organizational constraints.

This approach ensured the solution was not only visually improved, but also practical for long-term use by a small, distributed team.
{% endcapture %}

{% capture results %}
- Delivered a redesigned website that improved navigation, clarity, and user engagement  
- Launched a centralized knowledge hub that made JIL’s research and content easier to access and explore  
- Established an accessible, cohesive brand system aligned with WCAG standards  
- Enabled non-design staff to consistently create and maintain content using reusable templates and clear guidelines  
- Provided a complete, maintainable design system that JIL could independently sustain and evolve
