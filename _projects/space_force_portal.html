The team launched a minimum viable product (MVP), helping the United States Space Force (USSF) disseminate information. Servicemembers can use the portal to access resources like internal training opportunities, human resource documents, and announcements.

{% endcapture %} {% capture challenge %}

USSF lacked a central place to share announcements, resources, and other important information with servicemembers (Guardians), instead relying on easily missed emails or newsletters to disseminate information. It wasnâ€™t always clear where to get help when trying to answer a question about medical benefits, find career training opportunities, or access other important human resources information. Space Force needed a streamlined way to share information with teams and the broader organization, and so we needed to get to an MVP quickly. Before we could build excitement and trust in a more extensive portal, our immediate goal was to quickly launch a landing page.

{% endcapture %} {% capture solution %}

In partnership with USSF stakeholders, we launched an MVP portal in two months. We used lightweight design tools to quickly prototype page layouts for the microsite, with best practice design patterns from the U.S. Web Design System Rather than cluttering the portal with links, the team worked with USSF to identify the highest priority content needs. We started with announcements, human resources information, and career opportunities.

{% endcapture %} {% capture results %}

Our three-person Skylight team:

  • Launched an MVP portal in two months to share important information organization-wide
  • Partnered with Space Force to design an intuitive user experience, modern look and feel, and easy-to-find content
  • Set the foundation for the broader portal buildout
